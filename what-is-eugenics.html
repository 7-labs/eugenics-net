What Is Eugenics?

Eugenics was a movement that claimed human populations could be improved by controlling reproduction. This site treats it as pseudoscience, policy history, and a warning about how science can be misused.

Editorial owner: Eugenics History & Bioethics Project Last updated: May 30, 2026 Topics: definition, scientific racism, bioethics
Content warning: Discusses coercive reproduction policy, racism, ableism, and dehumanizing historical claims.

Project Position

This article defines eugenics to critique it. It does not present eugenic claims as legitimate science or as a neutral political option.

Definition

Eugenics was a social and political movement that claimed human populations could be improved by controlling who was encouraged, discouraged, or prevented from having children. Its advocates used the language of heredity, public health, statistics, and national improvement, but their claims were tied to racism, ableism, class prejudice, antisemitism, nativism, and other forms of hierarchy.

The National Human Genome Research Institute describes eugenics as a scientifically inaccurate theory. That point matters. Eugenics was not just a set of outdated opinions. It became a policy program that treated people as reproductive risks or assets rather than as rights-bearing persons.

Positive and Negative Eugenics

Historians often distinguish between “positive” and “negative” eugenics. Positive eugenics encouraged people labeled “fit” to reproduce. Negative eugenics tried to stop people labeled “unfit” from reproducing through marriage restrictions, segregation, institutionalization, sterilization, and other coercive systems.

Both forms depended on the same moral error: they treated human worth as something that could be ranked by heredity and managed by authorities.

Why the Term Is Dangerous

The word “eugenics” is not a neutral synonym for genetics, health, fertility care, or human improvement. It carries a history of forced sterilization, scientific racism, disability discrimination, immigration restriction, and Nazi racial hygiene. Reusing the word as a modern technology brand or lifestyle concept risks erasing that history.

This is why the site uses an explicit anti-endorsement statement. Documentation without ethical framing can become amplification. The goal here is not to make eugenics sound modern, balanced, or provocative. The goal is to explain why it was wrong, how it caused harm, and what modern science and public policy need to remember.

How to Study It Responsibly

Responsible study starts with definitions, sources, and context. Primary materials should not be shown as isolated curiosities. They need warnings, notes about harmful claims, explanation of affected communities, and discussion of why the material matters today.

When readers encounter eugenic language, they should ask: Who made this claim? What institution benefited? Which groups were targeted? What evidence was missing or misused? What rights were denied? Those questions turn a dangerous archive into an educational one.

FAQ

Is this site pro-eugenics?

No. The site documents and critiques eugenics, scientific racism, forced sterilization, and genetic discrimination.

Is eugenics the same as genetics?

No. Genetics is a scientific field. Eugenics was an ideology and policy movement that misused heredity and social prejudice to rank people and control reproduction.
