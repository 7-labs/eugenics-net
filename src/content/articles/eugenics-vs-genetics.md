## Genetics Is a Science

Genetics studies inheritance, genes, variation, development, and disease. It can support diagnosis, treatment, ancestry research, public health, and basic biological knowledge when practiced responsibly.

## Eugenics Was an Ideology

Eugenics used heredity claims to argue that authorities should shape the population by controlling reproduction. It turned social prejudice into policy and treated some people as less worthy of family life, citizenship, or survival.

That is why the distinction matters. Genetics can be evidence-based science. Eugenics was a hierarchy project.

## Where the Boundary Can Blur

Modern genetic tools can become ethically dangerous when they are used to rank social worth, pressure reproductive choices, treat disability as a problem to eliminate, or define racial groups as biological categories with fixed value.

The boundary is not only technical. It is about power, consent, access, and rights.

## A Better Public Vocabulary

Use precise terms. Say genetics when discussing inheritance. Say genetic counseling when discussing voluntary clinical guidance. Say genetic discrimination when describing unfair treatment. Reserve eugenics for the historical and ideological movement that used heredity to justify hierarchy and coercion.
